❄️ November – February

Winter Season
Disease Guide

Cold weather suppresses immunity and creates ideal conditions for respiratory infections to spread. Understand the risks and how to stay protected this winter.

🤧
Influenza (Flu)
Viral respiratory illness peaking in winter months
Causes
Symptoms
Prevention
  • Influenza A/B/C virus strains mutating annually
  • Airborne droplet transmission — sneezing, coughing
  • Crowded indoor environments with poor ventilation
  • Cold dry air reducing nasal mucus barrier effectiveness
  • Sudden high fever (38–40°C) with chills
  • Severe muscle aches and body pains
  • Dry cough and sore throat
  • Extreme fatigue and headache
  • Annual flu vaccination is the most effective measure
  • Wash hands regularly for at least 20 seconds
  • Wear a mask in crowded or enclosed spaces
  • Stay home when symptomatic to prevent spread
🫁
Pneumonia & Bronchitis
Lower respiratory tract infections common in cold months
Causes
Symptoms
Prevention
  • Streptococcus pneumoniae — the most common bacteria
  • Viral pneumonia following influenza infection
  • Cold air chronically irritating the bronchial lining
  • Weakened immunity in elderly and young children
  • Persistent productive cough with coloured phlegm
  • Sharp chest pain that worsens with breathing
  • High fever with rigours and sweating
  • Shortness of breath even at rest
  • Pneumococcal vaccine — recommended for 65+ and children
  • Keep warm; avoid cold drafts and damp environments
  • Seek medical attention early for any respiratory symptoms
  • Ensure good indoor air circulation and humidity
🤒
Common Cold & Sore Throat
Rhinovirus infections spreading rapidly in winter air
Causes
Symptoms
Prevention
  • Rhinovirus — responsible for most common colds
  • Contact with contaminated surfaces and then face-touching
  • Coronavirus and RSV strains in winter months
  • Cold dry air reducing nasal immune defences
  • Runny or blocked nose; frequent sneezing
  • Sore or scratchy throat
  • Mild fever and generalised fatigue
  • Cough developing after nasal symptoms begin
  • Wash hands for 20 seconds; sanitise frequently touched surfaces
  • Cover mouth and nose when sneezing — use elbow, not hands
  • Avoid sharing cups, utensils, or towels
  • Boost immunity: Vitamin C, zinc, and adequate sleep
😮‍💨
Asthma & Respiratory Flare-ups
Cold air and allergens triggering airway constriction
Causes
Symptoms
Prevention
  • Cold air acting as a direct bronchial irritant
  • Indoor dust mites and mould spores in closed rooms
  • Air pollution and vehicle emissions in winter fog
  • Respiratory infections triggering underlying asthma
  • Wheezing — a whistling sound when breathing
  • Tight chest and shortness of breath
  • Persistent nighttime or early morning cough
  • Blue-tinged lips in severe attacks — seek emergency care
  • Always carry prescribed inhaler — never skip doses
  • Avoid early morning outdoor exercise in cold air
  • Wrap a scarf over nose and mouth when outdoors
  • Use air purifiers indoors; maintain indoor humidity 40–50%
❄️ Winter Wellness Tips
💉
Vaccinate Early
Get your annual flu vaccine before November. Pneumococcal vaccine is strongly recommended for the elderly, children under 5, and immunocompromised individuals.
🧤
Layer Up Smart
Dress in breathable, moisture-wicking layers. Keep extremities warm — hands, feet, and neck are major heat-loss areas. Avoid going out with wet hair.
🌬️
Indoor Air Quality
Ventilate rooms for 10 min daily despite cold. Use air purifiers, avoid indoor smoking, and keep humidity between 40–50% to reduce viral transmission.
