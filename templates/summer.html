Summer Season
Disease Guide

High temperatures and water contamination define summer health risks in India. Learn the causes, symptoms, and prevention strategies for each condition.

🌡️
Heat Stroke & Exhaustion
Thermoregulation failure from excessive heat exposure
Causes
Symptoms
Prevention
  • Prolonged sun or high-temperature exposure
  • Outdoor work during peak heat (12–4 PM)
  • Inadequate hydration in hot conditions
  • High-humidity environments blocking sweat evaporation
  • Core body temperature above 40°C
  • Confusion, disorientation, slurred speech
  • Rapid, strong heartbeat
  • Hot, red skin — absence of sweating (heat stroke)
  • Avoid outdoor activity 12–4 PM in peak summer
  • Drink 3–4 litres of water daily; do not wait until thirsty
  • Wear light-coloured, loose, breathable clothing
  • Use wet cloth on neck and wrists to cool down quickly
🦠
Cholera & Gastroenteritis
Waterborne bacterial infections peaking in summer heat
Causes
Symptoms
Prevention
  • Vibrio cholerae bacteria in contaminated water
  • Uncooked or improperly stored food in heat
  • Poor sanitation and open-air food handling
  • Sharing water sources with infected individuals
  • Sudden onset of profuse, watery diarrhea
  • Severe vomiting leading to rapid dehydration
  • Muscle cramps and extreme weakness
  • Sunken eyes, dry mouth — shock in severe cases
  • Drink only boiled, filtered, or bottled water
  • Wash hands thoroughly before eating and after toilet use
  • Avoid raw street food in high-risk areas during summer
  • Begin ORS immediately if diarrhea starts
👁️
Conjunctivitis (Pink Eye)
Highly contagious eye infection surging in summer
Causes
Symptoms
Prevention
  • Adenovirus — spreads through direct contact
  • Bacterial infection via contaminated hands or surfaces
  • Swimming pool water with inadequate chlorination
  • Dust and UV exposure irritating the conjunctiva
  • Redness, itching, burning in one or both eyes
  • Watery or thick discharge; morning crusting
  • Sensitivity to bright light
  • Swollen eyelids and gritty feeling
  • Wash hands frequently; avoid touching eyes in public
  • Do not share towels, pillowcases, or eye drops
  • Wear UV-protection sunglasses outdoors
  • Seek antibiotic eye drops early for bacterial form
💧
Dehydration & Heat Cramps
Fluid and electrolyte loss from prolonged heat exposure
Causes
Symptoms
Prevention
  • Excessive sweating without adequate fluid replacement
  • Diarrhea or vomiting causing rapid fluid loss
  • Strenuous physical exertion in high heat
  • Electrolyte imbalance from sodium and potassium loss
  • Dark yellow or reduced urine output
  • Dizziness, fatigue, and dry mouth
  • Painful muscle spasms in legs and abdomen
  • Rapid heartbeat, confusion in severe cases
  • Drink water even when not feeling thirsty
  • Use ORS, coconut water, or electrolyte drinks
  • Avoid caffeinated and alcoholic beverages in heat
  • Rest in shade every 30–45 min during outdoor work
⚡ Summer Survival Tips
🕛
Timing Matters
Avoid direct sun exposure between 12 PM and 4 PM. Schedule outdoor activities for early morning or evening hours only.
🥤
Hydration First
Drink at least 3–4 litres daily. Include ORS, coconut water, or buttermilk. Keep a water bottle within arm's reach at all times.
🏠
Safe Food Habits
Eat freshly cooked food. Avoid street food stored in open air. Refrigerate leftovers promptly and never consume food left out for over 2 hours.
